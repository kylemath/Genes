üìñ Quick Tutorial: Download Your First Dataset

Step 1: Choose a Dataset

Click on "Access Dataset" button above (try the Australian Twin Study - it's easiest)

Step 2: Navigate to Downloads

On the GEO page, scroll down to find the "Supplementary file" section

Step 3: Download Files

Click on any .txt, .csv, or .xlsx file to download

Tip: Start with smaller files first!

Step 4: Open and Explore

Open the file in Excel, Google Sheets, or a text editor to see the data

Look for columns like:

  • gene_name or gene_id
  • chromosome
  • position
  • methylation_value or expression_level

Step 5: Extract DNA Sequences (if available)

Some datasets include actual sequences. Look for FASTA format data:

>Gene_Name or Sequence_ID
ATGCGTACGTAGCTAGCTAGCTAGCTAGCTA
GCTAGCTAGCTAGCTAGCTAGCTAGCTAGCT

Copy these sequences and paste into our DNA Analysis tool!

üîß Advanced: Getting Actual DNA Sequences

Note: Most twin studies focus on gene expression or methylation, not raw DNA sequences. For actual sequences, you'll need to use additional tools.

Option 1: Extract Specific Genes

Once you know which genes are interesting from the twin study, get their sequences:

Using NCBI Gene Database

  1. Go to NCBI Gene
  2. Search for your gene (e.g., "BRCA1 human")
  3. Click on the gene result
  4. Look for "Genomic regions, transcripts, and products" section
  5. Click "FASTA" to get the sequence
  6. Copy and paste into our analyzer!

Option 2: Use NCBI's E-Utilities (Automated)

For programmatic access, use NCBI's API:

# Example: Get insulin gene sequence
curl "https://eutils.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/entrez/eutils/efetch.fcgi?db=nuccore&id=NM_000207&rettype=fasta" > insulin.fasta

Option 3: Ensembl Browser

  1. Visit Ensembl
  2. Search for a gene
  3. Click "Export Data" or "Sequence"
  4. Select FASTA format
  5. Download and analyze!
